# Industry context

Use this information to have a better understanding of how the application must behave following the key concepts and workflows.

## Industry

Pharmaceutical

## Entities

### Medical Sales Representative (Visitador Médico)

Medical sales representatives act as the crucial link between manufacturers and healthcare professionals, selling pharmaceutical products, medical equipment, and devices. They work within an assigned territory, visiting hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies to educate doctors, nurses, and pharmacists, ultimately aiming to increase product usage and meet sales targets.

### Medical Sales District Manager (Gerente de Distrito)

A Medical Sales District Manager leads a team of sales representatives within a specific geographic area, driving revenue by achieving sales targets, implementing marketing strategies, and coaching reps on product knowledge and selling skills. They bridge corporate goals with field execution, managing budgets, recruiting talent, and building key stakeholder relationships.

## Medical Sales Product Manager (Gerente de Producto)

A Medical Sales Product Manager drives the commercial success of medical devices or pharmaceuticals by developing marketing strategies, analyzing market trends, and managing the product lifecycle. They bridge the gap between technical teams and sales, creating promotional materials, training sales reps, and setting pricing to maximize market share.

### Healthcare professional (Médico)

They serve as the key decision-makers and evaluators of new technologies. They collaborate with representatives to assess clinical data, run product trials, and receive in-person education regarding products, aiming to improve patient outcomes while integrating new solutions into their clinical workflows. 

### Tier (Segmento, Quintil, Tier)

Tiering is the strategic process of prioritizing a sales representative's time and resources by categorizing HCPs into groups based on their influence and potential value.
When a representative manages a specialty like cardiology, they don't treat all cardiologists the same. Instead, they use a data-driven model to assign them to tiers, typically ranging from Tier 1 (High Priority) to Tier 3 (Low Priority). 

### Panel of Healthcare professionals (Panel Médico)

During promotion visits, medical sales representatives target a tiered "panel" of professionals primarily composed of authorized prescribers and those who directly influence patient treatment plans.
By using this tiered panel, a representative ensures they aren't "reacting" to their territory but proactively focusing on the HCPs who drive the most patient outcomes and revenue. 

### Promotional Cycle (Ciclo de Visita)

The 22-day promotional cycle (representing approximately one business month) is a foundational tactical framework in pharmaceutical sales. This "Cycle Plan" ensures that the entire territory is covered systematically according to the strategic priorities set by District and Product Managers.

1. The Structure of the 22-Day Cycle
The cycle is designed around the standard number of working days in a month (excluding weekends). The goal is for the representative to execute a complete rotation of their assigned HCP Panel within this timeframe. 

    * Coverage vs. Frequency: The representative must balance "coverage" (visiting everyone on the panel at least once) with "frequency" (visiting high-priority Tier 1 HCPs multiple times, such as twice per cycle).
    * Daily Call Goal: To complete a panel of 200–250 HCPs in 22 days, reps typically aim for 10–12 calls per day.
    * Route Optimization: Planning is done geographically to minimize travel time and maximize time spent with HCPs. 

2. Strategic Alignment: The "Cycle Meeting"
Before the 22-day cycle begins, the sales force attends a Cycle Meeting where the strategy is dictated by two key roles:
    * Product Managers: They launch the "Cycle Campaign," providing the Core Message, visual aids, and promotional materials (clinical studies, brochures, or samples). They define which products take "Position 1" (primary focus) and "Position 2" (secondary focus) during the visit.
    * District Managers: They translate the national strategy into local execution. They set the KPIs for the 22 days, such as the required number of Tier 1 visits or the expected "strike rate" (prescriptions generated per visit). 

3. The "Promotional Grid"
The core of the cycle is a grid that dictates exactly which message is delivered to which HCP segment:
    * Tier 1 (High Potential): May receive two visits per cycle. The first visit might focus on a new clinical indication, while the follow-up visit (e.g., on Day 18) focuses on overcoming specific objections or closing the sale.
    * Tier 2/3 (Moderate/Low): Typically receive one visit per cycle focusing on a broader "maintenance" message or brand awareness. 

4. Workflow and Monitoring
    * Pre-Call Planning: Every morning, the rep reviews the specific objectives for that day’s HCPs, ensuring they have the correct samples and detailing tools.
    * Post-Call Reporting: Reps use CRM systems (like Veeva or IQVIA) to log activity daily. This allows District Managers to monitor the cycle's progress in real-time and ensure 100% panel coverage by Day 22
